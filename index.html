About Me

I'm Colin A. Campbell, someone who is inspired to create things that can make an impact on our society for the good, that will make things easier. I spent the early years of my life living in South St. Andrew, then I later moved to a community in South St. Catherine which is where I currently reside.

Between 2007 and 2013 I've attended Mico Practising Primary School. I graduated to my Alma Mater Jamaica College, which I spent 6 years, leaving in lower six. After Jamaica College I spent the last years in Highschool finishing up CAPE at Wolmers' Highschool for Boys. I'm currently studying computer science at The Univerisity of The West Indies (UWI) Mona Campus

After UWI I plan to work as a Software Engineer to gain experience while I work on my business, after which I will like to go fulltime in. My business Eazley is a software company aimed at optimizing the growth of entrepreneurs and small business owners through digital experiences, ie application development, consulting etc.

Few Of My Favourite Books

Book Name Author Overview
Start With Why Simon Sinek Long term growth comes when we have a reason behind what we do. One that can unite others to take action that can lead to what we want to accomplish.
Never Split The Difference Chris Voss Empathy is very powerful to not only understand people but also to understand how to guide other people's decision in negotiating and communicating overall.
Zero To One Peter Theil New things are hard to create, but creating something new is the only way to acquire innovations and avoid competition in business. 'Competition is for losers' in his own words.
Deception Point Dan Brown An exciting thriller that illustrates how selfish intentions can cause misguidance and misinformation. No matter how powerful someone is.
The Lean Startup Eric Ries The Learn Startup introduces us to a new way of running businesses, teams or projects. A more efficient way, or a "Lean" way. It outlines a methodology that is focused on product/customer development with a scientific approach.
The 50th Law Robert Greene & 50 cents The thoughts of both successful persons in one book take us through their mindset of being mindful of the intellect, spiritual, and emotional flow. It reminds us that because death is inevitable, taking careful approaches to anything in our life is no way to be successful. In essence, the Authors ask us to be mindful, confident, and fearless.
